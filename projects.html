Log-Periodic Antenna for THz Applications

2024–Present MS Research Project

Objective

Design, simulate, and fabricate planar log-periodic antennas optimized for terahertz frequency applications.

Methodology

Utilizing HFSS for electromagnetic simulation, followed by cleanroom fabrication using photolithography and metal deposition techniques. Antenna characterization performed using vector network analyzers and THz time-domain spectroscopy.

Key Achievements
Optimized radiation efficiency by 15%
Achieved broadband operation (0.1-1 THz)
Successfully integrated with on-chip THz sources

VLSI & Digital Design

RTL design, verification, and FPGA implementation projects

Router 1x3 RTL Design and Verification

2023 | VLSI Training Project

Overview: Designed and verified a packet router that accepts 8-bit data packets on a single input port and intelligently directs them to one of three output channels based on address decoding.

Implementation: Complete RTL design in Verilog/SystemVerilog with comprehensive testbench development. FPGA synthesis and validation performed on Xilinx Vivado and Intel Quartus Prime platforms.

Features: FIFO buffering, priority arbitration, error detection, and configurable packet size support.

Verilog SystemVerilog Xilinx Vivado Intel Quartus Prime FPGA

AHB-APB Bridge Protocol Verification

2023 | VLSI Training Project

Objective: Ensure complete protocol compliance of AHB-APB bridge design with ARM AMBA specifications for SoC integration.

Methodology: Developed comprehensive UVM-based verification environment with constrained-random stimulus generation, functional coverage models, and assertion-based verification.

Results: Achieved 100% functional coverage, identified and resolved critical corner-case bugs, validated timing closure for multi-master configurations.

UVM SystemVerilog QuestaSim AMBA Protocol Coverage Analysis

Quantum Computing

Quantum Random Number Generation

2021 | Bachelor Thesis

Research Focus: Exploiting fundamental quantum mechanical principles—superposition and entanglement—to generate cryptographically secure random numbers.

Implementation: Designed quantum circuits using Qiskit framework and executed on IBM Quantum processors. Developed quantum-to-classical mapping algorithms for practical applications.

Applications: Integrated QRNG with image encryption, steganography, and secure key generation protocols. Demonstrated superior randomness compared to classical pseudo-random generators through statistical testing (NIST, Diehard tests).
